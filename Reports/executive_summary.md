# Executive Summary
## Operations Funnel Analysis — B2B Talent Solutions Firm
**Period:** January 2023 – December 2024
**Prepared by:** Cristina Montenegro, Data Analyst
**Last updated:** May 2026

---
## Business Context
A mid-sized B2B Talent Solutions company operating across four acquisition channels, Referral, LinkedIn, Website, and Cold Outreach, lacked a unified view of how potential clients progressed through its sales pipeline. Decisions about channel investment, team focus, and process improvement were being made without a data foundation.

This analysis was conducted to answer three core questions:
- Where is the firm losing the most potential clients?
- How have KPIs evolved over 24 months?
- Which factors most strongly drive successful conversions?
---
## Key Findings

### 1. The funnel has two critical leak points
Of 380 leads tracked over 24 months, only 83 converted to closed deals, an overall conversion rate of 21.8%.

The two most significant loss points are:

**Early stage — Lead (35.5% of all leads)**
More than one in three leads never advanced past initial contact. This points to a qualification gap at entry: the firm is investing time in leads that are unlikely to convert from the first interaction.
**Late stage — Negotiation (16.8% of all leads)**
64 leads reached Negotiation — the final stage before 
closing — but did not convert. These represent the 
firm's most expensive losses: leads that consumed 
52–57 days of pipeline investment before stalling. 
The gap between a stalled Negotiation lead and a 
Closed_Won deal is just 3–7 days on average.

---
### 2. Referral converts best but LinkedIn drives the most revenue
| Channel | Conversion Rate | Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Referral | 31.6% | $131,184 |
| LinkedIn | 21.1% | $118,151 |
| Website | 18.9% | $69,438 |
| Cold Outreach | 12.3% | $28,912 |

Referral leads convert at nearly 2.6x the rate of Cold Outreach leads and carry the highest average deal value ($4,207). However, LinkedIn generates comparable revenue through volume — 133 leads vs Referral's 95.

Cold Outreach is the least efficient channel by every metric:lowest conversion rate, lowest deal value, and highest time investment per closed deal.

---
### 3. Operational efficiency improved significantly in 2024

Two positive trends emerged across the 24-month period:

**Conversion rate:** rose from ~20% average in 2023 to 
28.6% in Q4 2024: the strongest quarterly performance 
in the dataset.

**Sales cycle:** dropped from a peak of 62 days (Sep 2023) to 37 days (Nov 2024): a ~40% reduction in time-to-close.

Despite a slight decline in lead volume in late 2024, revenue remained stable confirming that the firm is closing higher-quality deals more efficiently.

---
### 4. Seasonal patterns are predictable but underutilized

The firm's highest-converting cohorts — May 2023 (43.8%), 
January 2023 (41.7%), and November 2023 (38.5%) align with classic B2B budget cycle triggers: post-holiday budget release, pre-summer urgency, and year-end spending.
These peaks did not repeat at the same intensity in 2024, suggesting the firm is not proactively capitalizing on predictable high-conversion windows.

---

## Recommendations

### Priority 1 — Intervene at Negotiation before day 21
**Impact: High | Effort: Low**
64 leads stalled at Negotiation after weeks of pipeline investment. Implementing a structured follow-up protocol at the 21-day mark such as, a check-in call, revised proposal, or decision deadline, could recover a meaningful share of these near-closes. Even a 15% recovery rate would add ~10 deals and approximately $42,000 in annual revenue.

### Priority 2 — Build a structured referral program
**Impact: High | Effort: Medium**
Referral is the firm's strongest channel but has the lowest volume (95 leads over 24 months). A structured referral incentive program,like targeting existing clients and professional networks, could scale the highest-ROI channel without increasing cost per lead.

### Priority 3 — Reassess Cold Outreach investment
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Low**
Cold Outreach generated $28,912 in revenue over 24 months at a 
12.3% conversion rate. Redirecting even 50% of that effort toward Referral development or LinkedIn content strategy would likely yield higher returns based on observed channel performance.

### Priority 4 — Build a calendar-based outreach strategy
**Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium**
The firm's best conversion months (January, May, November) follow predictable B2B budget cycle patterns. A proactive outreach push in the 4 weeks before these windows, targeting warm leads and past contacts, could systematically capture conversion peaks that currently appear only sporadically.
